Awards criterion for O-1A visa requires evidence that the individual has received nationally or internationally recognized prizes or awards for excellence in their field.

Examples include:
- Nobel Prize, Academy Award, Grammy, Emmy, or other major industry awards
- Pulitzer Prize, Fields Medal, or other prestigious academic awards
- Guggenheim Fellowship, MacArthur Fellowship, or other significant grants
- Industry-specific awards that demonstrate exceptional achievement
- Awards from recognized professional associations or societies
- National or international competitions in the applicant's field

The awards should be:
- Recognized nationally or internationally
- Prestigious within the field
- Based on excellence or achievement, not just participation
- Awarded through a competitive process

Documentation should include:
- Award certificates or letters
- Evidence of the award's significance and recognition
- Information about the awarding organization
- Media coverage of the award, if available
